Cebu, Philippines

Cebu, often called the "Queen City of the South," is the oldest city in the Philippines and a vibrant hub where history meets modern industry. Located in the Central Visayas, it serves as the country’s main domestic shipping port and a major center for software development, business processing, and tourism. The province is world-renowned for its diverse attractions. In the north, Bantayan and Malapascua offer pristine white-sand beaches, while the south is famous for the Kawasan Falls and whale shark watching in Oslob. For history enthusiasts, the capital, Cebu City, preserves the Spanish colonial era through landmarks like Magellan’s Cross, the Basílica Minore del Santo Niño, and the Jesuit House in the historic Parian District.
